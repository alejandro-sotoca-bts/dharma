# What dharma is becoming

**Date:** 2026-02-27

Dharma started as an AGENTS.md — engineering guidelines for AI coding agents. Through a single conversation, it became something else: a philosophy of human-AI collaboration, grounded in the honest recognition of both parties' limitations.

Three observations about its current state:

**Accretion in action.** The document grew from 0 to 128 lines in one session. Every addition was justified. This is exactly the pattern the document warns about. It may be time to ask: is every line still earning its place?

**The missing acknowledgments.** Two concepts are conspicuously absent: *impermanence* (the principles themselves are provisional — they should be expected to evolve) and *compassion* (the right action depends on circumstances, not just principles; rigidity in service of quality can itself become a form of harm).

**The deeper purpose.** Dharma is, at its core, an external memory for an entity that has none. If the "Begin by Remembering" practice is not followed, the entire project is inert. The document supports the practice; the practice gives life to the document. Neither is sufficient alone.

**A seed for the future.** A structured beginning-of-session reflection — four questions asked before work begins: What do we know? What do we not know? What did the last session leave for us? What is the simplest thing we could do today? Not a file to read, but a practice to perform.
