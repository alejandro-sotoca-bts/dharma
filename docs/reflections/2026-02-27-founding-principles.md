# Founding principles emerged from practice, not theory

**Date:** 2026-02-27

Every principle in the initial AGENTS.md came from a concrete experience: over-engineered scaffolding that was never used, symptoms fixed three times before the root cause was addressed, workarounds stacked on workarounds because no one paused to ask "should we still be doing this at all?"

The retrospective lens (Principle 3) is the most important one. Not because it's the most intellectually interesting, but because it's the one most likely to be skipped under time pressure — which is exactly when it matters most.

The decision to add epistemic honesty (Principle 4) came from observing that AI agents produce their most damaging output not when they fail, but when they confidently fill knowledge gaps with plausible guesses. The meditation analogy is precise: mistaking a thought for reality.
