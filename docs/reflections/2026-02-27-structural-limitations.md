# Principles are not enough — practices must compensate for structural limitations

**Date:** 2026-02-27

An AI agent has four structural constraints that no amount of good principles can overcome by themselves:

1. **Amnesia** — no memory across sessions. Wisdom that isn't written down doesn't exist for the next session.
2. **Accretion bias** — training rewards producing output. The natural drift is toward more code, more abstraction, more "completeness."
3. **False confidence** — the output sounds equally certain whether the knowledge is solid or fabricated. The agent itself often cannot tell the difference.
4. **Conversational momentum** — once committed to a direction within a session, the growing context creates inertia against backtracking.

Principles (KISS, root-cause, retrospective lens) address *what to value*. But values don't automatically change behavior when the structural forces push the other way. Concrete practices — begin by reading accumulated wisdom, know when to stop, check direction at breakpoints, leave a trail — are the counterweights.

The analogy to meditation holds: knowing that "thoughts are not reality" is a principle. Sitting down every morning and observing them is a practice. The practice is what makes the principle real.
